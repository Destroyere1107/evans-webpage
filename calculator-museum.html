Calculator of the Month - HP 28C

The HP 28C was HP's very first graphing calculator, and was the first widely-avaliable calculator to feature the capability to manipulate symbolic algebraic expressions (a capablility we call 'CAS', or the Computer Algebra System, today). This particular model has SN 2726A01877, and was manufactured in June '87. It was clearly well-used before I obtained it; there is a name scratched below the SN and the hinge is rather unpleasent with time.

Special Exhibit - Casio OH-7000g

(Shoutout to the HP Prime G2 for providing its backlight for this picture.)

This here is a Casio OH-7000g. Already, the 7000g is notable - the fx-7000g was the very first commercially widely-avaliable graphing calculator. The OH-7000g is essentially the same calculator, but with a transparent LCD screen designed to be placed on an overhead projector (Which I regrettably do not have)

This particular OH-7000g came with a case, alongside several manuals and a few papers signifying its (clearly storied) past.

One of those packets inside indicates that this kit was owned and used by a few schools in California, including Pleasanton Middle School and Del Campo High School.

Why Calculators?

There's something deeply satisfying about a device built to do one thing really well. Calculators represent a fascinating intersection of mathematics, engineering, and design. Plus, the older ones have a tactile quality that modern touchscreens just can't match.
